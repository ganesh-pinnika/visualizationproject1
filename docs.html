Dr Snow's Map

Visualization

Dr Snow's Map Documentation

This is a visualization of the 1854 cholera outbreak in London. The visualization is based on the data from the original paper by John Snow.

The visualization is built using D3.js


Dr. John Snow’s map established a relationship between cholera and contaminated water during London’s 1854 cholera epidemic. In this project, I try to recreate that map using modern tools and processes, javascript and d3.js, to be viewed on webpages. Using the datasets of street mappings, pump locations, deathdays of victims, and the ages and gender of these victims, I use d3.js to manipulate the data into a visual representation.


With the given datasets, some of the rational relationships to establish are how the position of the pumps relates to the number of deaths in the area, how the deaths occur across a range of ages, and the number of deaths per period during the epidemic. To make those relationships, a map, a horizontal bar chart, and a pie chart were chosen as the visualizations of choice.



On the map, I plotted the various pumps within the street and also the deaths across the street, differentiating between males and females. The horizontal bar chart seeks to show how many deaths occur per day during the said period. On the x axis, the number of deaths and the date on the y axis. The bars are filled with red to indicate danger(death). The number of deaths are also indicated at the end of the bars. The bar chart also interacts with the map when a bar in hovered over. The map shows how many deaths occured on the hovered item. Since the dataset for deaths don’t include the date of death, the map merely shows the number of deaths that corresponds to the hovered bar.
